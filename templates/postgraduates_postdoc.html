Career Options for Post-Doctoral Researchers

Research and Academic Careers

As a Post-Doctoral researcher, you have a wide range of career options in academia and research. Here are some popular career paths:

University Professor

Teach undergraduate and graduate students, conduct research, publish papers, and mentor students in your field of expertise. Lead academic departments and research groups.

Research Scientist

Lead research projects in industry, government, or non-profit organizations. Develop new products, technologies, or knowledge in your specialized field.

Research Director

Oversee research departments or programs in academic institutions, research organizations, or industry. Set research priorities and manage research teams.

Science Policy Advisor

Work with government agencies to develop policies based on scientific evidence and research findings. Bridge the gap between science and policy-making.

Scientific Consultant

Provide specialized scientific expertise to businesses, government agencies, or other organizations. Solve complex problems and develop innovative solutions.

Science Communicator

Translate complex scientific information for general audiences. Work in science journalism, publishing, museums, or public outreach programs.

Professional Development Options

After completing your Post-Doctoral research, you might consider these professional development paths:

  • Advanced research fellowships in prestigious institutions
  • Leadership and management training for research roles
  • Specialized technical certifications in your field
  • Entrepreneurship training for research commercialization
